Risk Management

Part 1: Intro & Identification

💡 Introduction:

Risk management in cybersecurity is the process of identifying, analyzing, and reducing potential security risks that may affect systems, networks, or data. Organizations use this to minimize damage before an attack happens.

Common Risk Types

  • Operational Risks
    • Human Errors (e.g., accidental data deletion).
    • Internal Process Failures (e.g., poor workflow).
  • Technical Risks
    • Software Vulnerabilities (e.g., unpatched bugs).
    • Outdated Systems (e.g., legacy hardware).
  • Financial Risks
    • Direct Loss of Money (e.g., ransomware payments).
    • Operational Downtime (e.g., loss of business hours).
  • Compliance Risks
    • Regulatory Fines (e.g., GDPR violations).
    • Legal Actions (e.g., lawsuits after data breach).

The Risk Management Cycle: Identify → Assess → Mitigate → Monitor.
